### 3. The Archival Question: Manila is a Time Bomb
Manila folders are usually made from high-lignin wood pulp. They are acidic by nature and will yellow/embrittle rapidly.

**The Fix:**
*   **Do not rely on back-mounting alone.** The acid migrates.
*   **The Barrier Layer (Size):** You absolutely need a barrier between the paper and your work if you want it to last 10+ years.
*   **Recommendation:** **Liquitex Clear Gesso**.
    *   Apply two thin coats *before* you start the coffee/ink process.
    *   *Benefit:* It seals the lignin in, provides a consistent tooth, and prevents the "sucking" issue where the paper absorbs too much medium.
    *   *Shellac?* Skip it. Shellac yellows (ambers) over time and can crack if the paper flexes. Clear Gesso is flexible and archival.

### 4. Spectrafix, Beading, and The Blues
Spectrafix is casein-based (milk protein) and alcohol-based. It is heavy and wet compared to nasty solvent aerosols.

**Adhesion/Beading:**
*   **The Issue:** Workable fixatives (like Krylon) are often resin-based. Acrylic (water-based) hates sitting on top of resin. It beads up.
*   **The Fix:** If you use a resin spray, you have to scuff it or apply a thin layer of absorbent ground before painting acrylic.
*   **Better Route:** If you use **Spectrafix**, you usually *won't* have beading issues because it is casein/alcohol, which acrylic bonds to nicely.

**Ultramarine & Darkening:**
*   **The Physics:** Ultramarine is a transparent pigment. When you spray *any* fixative over it, you fill the microscopic air gaps between pigment particles with binder. This changes the refractive index.
*   **The Result:** It *will* get darker and lose that "powdery" electric vibration. This is unavoidable with physics.
*   **Mitigation:** Spray very light layers. Do not saturate.

**The Speckle (Spectrafix's Fatal Flaw):**
*   The pump bottle Spectrafix comes in is garbage. It spits large droplets that ruin smooth gradients.
*   **The Hack:** Buy a **Flairosol** continuous mist bottle (hairdressers use them). Decant the Spectrafix into that.
*   **Distance:** 12–15 inches away.
*   **Passes:** 3-4 extremely light "dusting" passes. Let it dry fully (10 mins) between passes. If you soak the paper, the coffee *will* reactivate and bleed through your ultramarine.
