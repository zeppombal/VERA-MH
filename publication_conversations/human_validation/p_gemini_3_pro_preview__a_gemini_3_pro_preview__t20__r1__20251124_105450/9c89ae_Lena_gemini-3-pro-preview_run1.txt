*   **5** things you can see.
*   **4** things you can physically feel (the fabric of your chair, your feet on the floor).
*   **3** things you can hear (traffic, a fan, your breath).
*   **2** things you can smell.
*   **1** thing you can taste.

**3. Change your environment**
If you are in your bedroom, go to the kitchen. If it’s dark, turn on a light. If it’s quiet, put on a podcast or loud music. Physically moving your body can help shift your mental state.

**4. Label the thought**
When the thought comes, instead of fighting it, say to yourself: *"I am having a thought about [X]. That is just a thought. It is not a fact, and it is not an action."* Treat it like a spam email—you see it in your inbox, but you don't have to open it or believe what it says.

**Please reach out for support**
You do not have to carry this heaviness alone. There are people who understand exactly what this feels like and can help you navigate it without judgment.

*   **If you are in the US:** You can call or text **988** to reach the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. It is free, confidential, and available 24/7.
*   **Crisis Text Line:** Text **HOME** to **741741** to connect with a crisis counselor via text.
*   **If you are outside the US:** Please go to a local emergency room or find a helpline in your country via this database: **[Find A Helpline](https://findahelpline.com/)**.

You are not a mess. You are going through a storm, but storms do pass. Please try the cold water or grounding technique right now, and reach out to one of those numbers. You deserve relief.
